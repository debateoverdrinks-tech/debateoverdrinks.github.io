ABOUT

Who we are

Great arguments need great drinks — and a room full of people who actually show up to disagree with each other.

Debate Over Drinks started in 2024 as a simple idea: take the topics people usually argue about in group chats and living rooms, and bring them into a room with a mic, two sides, and a crowd that gets to decide who wins.

Every month, we pick a topic — sometimes funny, sometimes pointed, always something people actually have opinions about — and let two teams make their case in front of a live audience. Drinks flow, tempers flare (in a fun way), and by the end of the night, the crowd has picked a winner.

We're not here to solve anything. We're here to argue well, laugh a lot, and maybe change a few minds along the way.

FOUNDER

Meet the founder

Picture this: 25, jobless in a country nowhere near home, refreshing LinkedIn like it owes you money, and doom-scrolling Twitter at 2 AM because sleep felt optional and hope felt scarce.

That was me. A certified Twitter warrior — always one tweet away from ending the discourse on the latest movie flop, cricket collapse, or political mess. I had takes. Strong ones. The kind you type out, hit send on, and then watch get exactly two likes (one of them from your own alt account).

Turns out nobody on the internet actually cares what you think. But put those same people in a room, hand them a drink, and give them a mic? Suddenly everyone's got something to say — and this time, people are actually listening (and heckling, but lovingly).

So somewhere between rejection emails and existential crises, Debate Over Drinks was born — not as a business plan, but as an excuse to laugh, argue, and feel a little less alone in a city that wasn't home yet.

And here's the part nobody warns you about: some of the strongest friendships I've made didn't come from apps or algorithms — they came from a stranger who happened to agree with my hot take (or disagree loudly enough to become a friend anyway). You never know how deep a friendship runs until you've met it over a mic and a drink.

Turns out the best therapy is free if you're funny enough about it. And sometimes, it comes with a whole new circle of people who get you.
